02 — Cryptocurrency Risk

Cryptocurrency Risks

Cryptocurrency assets are highly speculative and volatile. Trading them carries substantial risks including but not limited to:

  • Extreme price volatility: Crypto asset prices can fluctuate by 20–80% or more within hours. There is no price floor — an asset's value can fall to zero.
  • Transaction irreversibility: Blockchain transactions are final. Sending crypto to a wrong address, wrong network, or after a scam cannot be reversed by DoronX or anyone else.
  • Network risk: Blockchain networks can experience congestion, forks, 51% attacks, or protocol-level failures outside DoronX's control.
  • Liquidity risk: During periods of high volatility, it may be difficult to buy or sell crypto at expected prices. Spreads can widen significantly.
  • Regulatory risk: Governments may restrict, ban, or heavily regulate cryptocurrency in your jurisdiction at any time, potentially making your holdings illiquid or worthless.
  • Scam & phishing risk: The crypto ecosystem is target-rich for scammers. DoronX will never ask for your private keys, seed phrases, or passwords. Always verify you are on the official app.
Network Selection Warning

Always double-check that you are sending or receiving on the correct network (BEP20, ERC20, TRC20, Solana, or BTC). Sending tokens on the wrong network will result in permanent, unrecoverable loss of funds.

03 — DeFi & Vault Risk

DeFi Protocol & Vault Risks

The DoronX Vault integrates with AAVE v3 on Polygon to generate yield on USDC deposits. While AAVE is one of the most established DeFi protocols, using it carries significant risks:

  • Smart contract risk: AAVE v3 and Polygon smart contracts, like all blockchain code, may contain bugs or vulnerabilities that could result in partial or total loss of deposited funds, even after audits.
  • Variable yield: APY rates displayed in the app are indicative and change in real time based on supply and demand. Displayed rates are not guaranteed and may fall to near zero.
  • Stablecoin depeg risk: USDC is a stablecoin designed to maintain a $1.00 peg. In extreme market conditions, stablecoins have depegged, causing losses. DoronX does not guarantee USDC's peg.
  • Polygon network risk: Validator failures, governance attacks, or bridge failures on the Polygon network could affect your deposited funds.
  • Withdrawal delay: Under extreme market conditions, liquidity in AAVE pools may be reduced, temporarily preventing or delaying withdrawals.
Vault APY Disclosure

The APY shown in your Vault dashboard reflects current protocol rates net of DoronX's management fee. This rate can change at any time. Historical returns do not predict future performance.

05 — Operational Risk

Platform & Operational Risks

  • Service availability: DoronX aims for maximum uptime but cannot guarantee uninterrupted access. Scheduled and unscheduled maintenance may affect service availability.
  • Transaction delays: On-chain transactions depend on blockchain confirmation times outside our control. During high network congestion, confirmation may take significantly longer than usual.
  • Security incidents: Despite robust security measures, no platform is immune to security incidents. A successful attack could affect your funds or personal data.
  • Device risk: If your device is compromised (malware, rooted), attackers may gain access to your DoronX account. Use only trusted devices and keep your OS updated.
  • Human error: Entering wrong amounts, wrong addresses, or confirming unintended transactions. Always review transaction details carefully before confirming.

  • Issuer risk: Virtual cards are issued through Maplerad's card programme. Changes to their programme, banking relationships, or regulatory status may affect card availability.
  • Merchant acceptance: Not all merchants accept virtual cards. Some merchants may block virtual or prepaid cards by policy.
  • Currency conversion: Transactions in currencies other than USD will be converted at the then-current exchange rate plus any applicable conversion fee.
  • Card security: You are responsible for keeping your card details secure. Unauthorised transactions should be reported immediately for dispute resolution.
  • Fund availability: Card funds are not bank deposits and are not covered by deposit insurance. In the event of Maplerad's insolvency, recovery of funds may be uncertain.
